About

The fifth studio album by David Bowie released in June of 1972

"Described as a loose concept album and rock opera, Ziggy Stardust is about Bowie's titular alter ego Ziggy Stardust, a fictional androgynous rock star who is sent to Earth as a saviour before an impending apocalyptic disaster. In the story, Ziggy wins the hearts of fans but suffers a fall from grace after succumbing to his own ego. The character was inspired by numerous musicians. Most of the album's concept was developed after the songs were recorded. The lyrics discuss the artificiality of rock music, political issues, drug use, sexual orientation and stardom."

David Bowie worked with Ken Scott and the Spiders from Mars to create the album
